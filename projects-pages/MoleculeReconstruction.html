3D Molecule Reconstruction Research

Mathematics Research

Research into the mathematical theory of and algorithms for 3D molecular structure determination using cryo-EM.


This research consists of developing the mathematical theory of and efficient algorithms for reconstructing 3D models of molecular particles via analysis of datasets produced by cryo-electron microscopy. In other words, we attempt to reconstruct 3D molecules from noisy 2D cryo-EM projections, using tools from algebraic geometry and group theory to studying the geometry and symmetry of the produced datasets.


Although this topic interests me greatly and I have learned a lot while reading about it, I'm planning on beefing up on my group theory and algebraic geoemtry skills before I devote serious time to the theoretical aspect of this project. For those interested, here are some great resources for learning more about this research topic:


  • M. van Heel, B. Gowen, R. Matadeen, E. V. Orlova, R. Finn, T. Pape, D. Cohen, H. Stark, R. Schmidt, M. Schatz, A. Patwardhan. Single-particle electron cryo-microscopy: towards atomic resolution, Q Rev Biophys, 33 (4), pp. 307-369 (2000).

    Excellent review paper serving as an introduction to the research surrounding cryo-EM.


  • J. Frank Three-Dimensional Electron Microscopy of Macromolecular Assemblies, New York: Oxford University Press, ISBN 0-19-518218-9 (2006).

    Great introductory text on reconstructing 3D macromolecular structures via cryo-EM methods.


  • MIT Technology Review article: An Algorithm Set To Revolutionize 3-D Protein Structure Discovery

    Non-technical article on the algorithm developed by Brubaker, Punjani, Fleet for 3D molecular reconstruction from noisy 2D cryo-EM images.


  • M. A. Brubaker, A. Punjani, D. J. Fleet. Building Proteins in a Day: Efficient 3D Molecular Reconstruction Preprint - arXiv (2015)

    Paper associated with the above MIT Technology Review article. Great read!

  • M. A. Brubaker, A. Punjani, D. J. Fleet. Building Proteins in a Day: Efficient 3D Molecular Reconstruction, techtalks.tv, Video (2015).


    15 minute tech talk by the authors of the aforementioned paper on the aforementioned topic.

  • A. Singer, Y. Shkolnisky. Three-Dimensional Structure Determination from Common Lines in Cryo-EM by Eigenvectors and Semidefinite Programming, SIAM Journal on Imaging Sciences, 4 (2), pp. 543-572 (2011).


  • A. Singer. Introduction to Three Dimensional Structure Determination of Macromolecules by Cryo-Electron Microscopy, Princeton University, Slides (2011).


  • R. Hadani, A. Singer. Representation Theoretic Patterns in Three Dimensional Cryo-Electron Microscopy I - The Intrinsic Reconstitution Algorithm, Annals of Mathematics, 174 (2), pp. 1219-1241 (2011).


  • R. Hadani, A. Singer. Representation Theoretic Patterns in Three Dimensional Cryo-Electron Microscopy II - The Class Averaging Problem, Foundations of Computational Mathematics (FoCM), 11 (5), pp. 589-616 (2011).

  • S. Gurevich, R. Hadani, A. Singer. Representation Theoretic Patterns in Three Dimensional Cryo-Electron Microscopy III - Presence of Point Symmetries Preprint

  • S. Gurevich. Applications to Three-Dimensional Structuring of Molecules Research Summary pg. 8-10


    The first section of the "Applied Mathematics" portion of Professor Gurevich's research summary is a great introduction to the mathematical framework of 3D molecular determination via cryo-EM, as well as a nice overview of the advantages of using considerations from algebraic geometry, group theory, and representation theory to exploit the symmetry and geometry of molecules.

  • G. Katsevich, A. Katsevich, A. Singer. Covariance Matrix Estimation for the Cryo-EM Heterogeneity Problem, SIAM Journal on Imaging Sciences, 8 (1), pp. 126-185 (2015).


    Interesting paper that proposes an estimator of the covariance matrix of a 3D molecule, estimated from noisy projections of the molecule. Such an estimated covariance matrix provides us with the leading eigenvalues of the covariance matrix, which could potentially be used solve the cryo-EM heterogeneity problem: mapping the space of conformational states of the molecule.

  • A. Singer. Covariance Matrix Estimation for the Cryo-EM Heterogeneity Problem | Amit Singer, Princeton University, Youtube video (2013)


    Great talk at Princeton given by Amit Singer, based upon the paper listed above.

  • ASPIRE team, Princeton University. Algorithms for Single Particle Reconstruction - Software toolbox for single-particle molecular structure determination from cryo-EM images, developed by researchers at Princeton. Landing page contains brief introductory exposition of the general topic. Here is the team's publications on the topic.

  • Wiki: Repository of 3D EM Methods


    Check this out for an incredibly extensive repository of three-dimensional electron microscopy methods!
