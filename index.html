Clark Zinzow

Web Developer, Software Engineer, & Aspiring Mathematician

I'm a 4th year undergraduate at the University of Wisconsin - Madison majoring in Mathematics and Computer Science. I have a passion for learning new skills and building cool, useful tools that make the user's life easier. My passion for app development stemmed from trying to find a certain application that would make some aspect of my life a lot easier and, far too often, I was not able find an appropriate tool. So, I decided that I would build it myself! Necessity often breeds creativity. This led to my obsession of building tools for filling efficiency/utility gaps in systems, no matter the what the system or what the target market. This desire to optimize the efficiency/utility of processes can most likely be traced to my mathematics, engineering, and physics background, which has proved to be extremely useful when approaching difficult problems.

Although I do love to develop apps, my first love will always be research. I'm in the process of multiple research projects in mathematics under the direction of Professor Jordan Ellenberg, and I am currently seeking a professor to oversee a research project in the area of partial differential equations. This research, in all likelihood, will be in the area of studying conditions in which a compact self-adjoint Hilbert-Schmidt class operator destroys the purely absolutely continuous spectrum of a self-adjoint operator with purely absolutely continuous spectrum. I am also looking for a professor to oversee research in the area of smoothed analysis of algorithms. I hope to complete the research for each of these topics by the time that I graduate in December of 2015.
